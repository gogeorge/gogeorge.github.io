If you read the list and ignore everything that is in brackets as well as the fourth point then that is a solid (but a bit vague) plan. However when looking at the list as a whole there are several contradictions:

- Cut Goverment Spending but also build a huge wall (aprox. $35 billion)
- Cut spending in the Education Department or any non-military department and expect more jobs

I am a bit confusing as to what I want Donald Trump to do. Do I want him to keep his promise on building a wall and placing huge tariffs or do I want Donald Trump to not initiate those two actions? Unfortunately both occasions have negative consequences.

The former will increase the Government Spending since building a wall is expensive. Now increasing the tariffs is a good idea, but increasing them by 50% is an unwise exaggeration. A car that was made in China when exported to America costed $10 000 will now cost $15 000 (same example applies to smartphones that are also made in China).

The latter will mean that Donald Trump did not keep his promise. Economically this could be a benefit but not keeping a promise means that Donald Trump talked about planning to build a wall and add huge tariffs in the future so the citizens will like it and vote for him and after winning the elections he changes his plans/aims. I hope this does not happen because after Donald Trump, any President could exaggerate on how they will improve America and then after the elections they will not keep their promise and switch to a completely different agenda/plan.
